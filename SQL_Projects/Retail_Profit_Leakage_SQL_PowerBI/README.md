# Retail Profit Leakage & Delivery Efficiency Analysis

This project uses SQL and Power BI to analyze retail performance and identify where profit is being lost.

The focus is not just on reporting sales, but on answering a practical business question:

Why is revenue strong while profit remains inconsistent?

## Business Problem

The business generates solid sales, but some categories, products, regions, and order patterns reduce overall profitability.

This project aims to:

- measure core sales and profit KPIs
- identify the main sources of profit leakage
- analyze the impact of discounts, shipping, and product mix
- highlight the highest-priority areas for action
- create reporting outputs to support decisions

## What I Built

- Cleaned and analyzed retail order data using SQL
- Built KPI and trend analysis for sales, profit, margin, discounts, and shipping
- Identified loss-making sub-categories, products, and customer segments
- Used Pareto analysis to isolate the few drivers behind most losses
- Prepared reporting-ready outputs for Power BI

## Skills Demonstrated

- SQL
- Data cleaning and validation
- Aggregations and KPI logic
- CTEs
- Window functions
- Business analysis
- Root cause analysis
- Reporting and dashboard thinking
- Power BI

## Key Insights

- Profitability is uneven across the business
- A small number of categories and products drive a large share of losses
- Higher discount levels are strongly linked to lower profitability
- Some segments and regions generate revenue without enough profit contribution
- Clear KPI and drilldown reporting makes profit leakage easier to act on
